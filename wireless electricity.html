Wireless Electricity Transmission

1. Introduction

Wireless electricity refers to the transmission of electrical energy without physical conductors, using electromagnetic fields. First conceptualized by Nikola Tesla, it has modern applications in wireless charging and smart devices.

2. Objective

To demonstrate a basic prototype of wireless electricity transfer using inductive coupling, and to understand the key principles of energy transfer without wires.

3. Project Overview

The prototype uses electromagnetic induction to transfer power from a primary to a secondary coil, lighting a fluorescent tube without any wire connection.

4. Methodology

  1. Coil Winding: Primary coil with 3 turns and a secondary coil with several turns were wound on opposite ends of a PVC pipe.
  2. Power Source: A DC battery powered the primary coil.
  3. Tube Light Placement: The tube was placed near the secondary coil to glow by induced voltage.

5. Working Principle

Using mutual induction, current in the primary coil creates a magnetic field. This induces voltage in the nearby secondary coil, lighting the tube without direct contact.

6. Results and Observations

  • Wireless power transfer was successful.
  • Tube light glowed without physical connection.
  • Brightness varied with coil alignment and distance.

7. Outcomes & Learnings

  • Built a functioning wireless energy demo.
  • Gained practical knowledge in inductive circuits.
  • Understood mutual inductance and coil behavior.

8. Future Scope

  • Use high-frequency AC for better efficiency.
  • Implement resonant coupling for longer range.
  • Apply this for wireless charging systems.
