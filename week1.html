Week 1: Laser-Cut Geodesic Sphere

1. Overview

This project involves designing a geodesic sphere using Fusion 360, composed of 120 triangular faces. The model is symmetrical and ideal for beginners learning 3D modeling and laser cutting.

2. Designing in Fusion 360

Start a new design in Fusion 360. Create an equilateral triangle using the polygon tool, constrain the dimensions, and prepare a flat layout. Use pattern or mirror tools to replicate triangles.

3. Exporting for Laser Cutting

Finish your sketch and export it as a DXF file. DXF is required by most laser cutter software. Make sure all lines are closed and use proper layer naming if required by your laser system.

4. Choosing Material: MDF

MDF is cheap and great for laser cutting. It cuts smoothly and provides a solid, clean surface. Use 3mm MDF for strong yet manageable thickness.

5. Laser Cutting

Load the DXF into your laser cutter software. Adjust power/speed based on your material (e.g., 60% power, 15mm/s). Make a test cut first, then cut all 120 triangles.

6. Assembly

Use glue (preferably wood glue or Fevicol) and begin assembling triangles from a flat cluster. Slowly shape into a dome and finally close it into a sphere. Alignment is critical.

7. Final Thoughts

This geodesic sphere introduces you to CAD, digital fabrication, and geometric structure. It's a fantastic learning tool for prototyping, architecture, and design fundamentals.
