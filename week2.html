Week 2: System Thinking - Component Disassembly

1. Overview

This week, we explored System Thinking by disassembling a physical product and analyzing each component’s function. The goal was to understand how parts interrelate and contribute to the system's whole functionality.

2. Key Highlights

  • Component-Level Learning: Dissected each part to study its material and function.
  • System Integration: Mapped how components interact to achieve collective goals.
  • Observation Skills: Identified relationships and potential areas of improvement.
  • Visual Tools: Used flowcharts and sketches to represent interactions.

3. Learning Process

The session began with an introduction to System Thinking fundamentals. Afterward, we dismantled the chosen device step-by-step. Each part was examined for:

  • Its individual function
  • Material used
  • Connectivity to other parts

After studying, we reassembled the system. This reinforced understanding of integration, sequencing, and design logic.

4. Insights Gained

From this hands-on activity, we discovered the value of:

  • Analyzing small parts to understand bigger systems
  • How minor design tweaks affect the whole function
  • Reverse engineering as a method for idea generation

5. Conclusion

System Thinking is more than just breaking things down — it's about understanding how everything works together. This exercise enhanced our skills in observation, analysis, and system integration—fundamental abilities for future engineers and designers.
