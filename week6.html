Week 6: Robotic Arm Design using Fusion 360

Objective

To design a robotic arm that demonstrates joint articulation and motor-driven motion, simulating real-world mechanical systems using Fusion 360.

Overview

This week, I focused on designing a robotic arm in Fusion 360. The aim was to simulate motorized movement and explore how articulated joints interact in realistic scenarios.

  • Planned the structural layout of the robotic arm
  • Designed parts: base, joints, arms, gripper
  • Applied joint constraints for motion simulation
  • Tested rotational and linear motion
  • Ensured designs are manufacturable and functional

Tools & Software

  • Fusion 360: For 3D CAD, motion simulation, and applying joint constraints
  • Mechanical Design Knowledge: To support torque and structural logic
  • SolidWorks: (Conceptual reference only)

Key Learnings

  • Created CAD components optimized for robotics
  • Learned realistic joint motion planning
  • Understood constraint-based simulation
  • Practiced designing with manufacturing and usability in mind

Outcome

Successfully modeled a functional robotic arm capable of basic movements. Integrated joints and motors virtually to simulate practical mechanics. This expanded my CAD and design-thinking capabilities.

Reflection

This project deepened my understanding of how design thinking applies to robotics. CAD tools like Fusion 360 helped turn concepts into visual, testable prototypes. I look forward to exploring more complex robotic systems and improving through practice.
