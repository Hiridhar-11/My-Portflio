Week 5: Applied Design Thinking – All-Terrain Table

1. Introduction

This project focused on creating a user-centric table design through empathy-driven design thinking. Led by Mr. Deepak, the session emphasized structured problem-solving and iterative prototyping.

2. Challenges Faced

  • Understanding user needs
  • Adaptability across environments
  • Material selection and durability
  • Ergonomics and comfort
  • Structural stability vs portability
  • Manufacturing feasibility
  • Iterative design validation

3. Step-by-Step Process

1. Empathy & Research

We conducted user interviews and on-site observations to gather pain points about traditional table usage in dynamic environments.

2. Ideation & Concept Development

Brainstormed multiple designs and selected the “All-Terrain Table” as the most versatile and user-adaptive concept.

3. Sketching & Initial Prototyping

Created low-fidelity sketches to explore form and structure. Peer and mentor feedback guided early design decisions.

4. 3D Modeling & Digital Prototype

Used Fusion 360 to model the table with focus on adaptability, aesthetics, and structural integrity.

5. Testing & Iteration

Tested the prototype’s usability, gathered feedback, and refined the model through multiple design loops.

6. Final Design & Documentation

The All-Terrain Table was finalized with features addressing stability, portability, and user experience. The design journey was documented for future development.

4. Key Takeaways

  • Empathy leads to relevant, impactful innovation.
  • Iteration improves both function and form.
  • Tools like Fusion 360 accelerate prototyping.
  • Real feedback ensures real usability.
  • Cross-disciplinary teamwork drives creativity.
  • Consider feasibility right from the concept stage.

5. Conclusion

This session strengthened our understanding of user-driven design, structured development, and real-world problem-solving through a practical, hands-on approach.
